# 1. Types of Learning
- There are 4 types of learning: **Associative**, **Observational**, **Experiential**, and **Abstract**.
## 1.1. Associative
- This type of learning is a very primitive form. It can be easily observed in most animals like dogs and cats.
- It is formed by linking 2 concepts together.
- It is an adaption to a condition or situation and a reponse or stimuli to it.
### 1.1.1. Classical Conditioning
- In this, an organism learns to associate a *external stimulus* with a biological stimulus, resulting in a conditioned response.
- For example, ringing a bell and giving food to dog. When you ring the bell, the dog starts to salivate even before the food is served, as it associates the bell with food. This experiment is known as **Pavlov's Dog Experiment**.
### 1.1.2. Operant Conditioning
- In this, an organism learns to associate a *behaviour* with a consequence, resulting in a conditioned response.
- For example, a rat in a maze learns to press a lever to get food. The rat associates the lever press with food, and thus presses the lever more often.
