- **Temperature** is a measure of the **average kinetic energy** of the particles.
- Heat flows from a **higher temperature body to a lower temperature body** until thermal equilibrium is reached.
- Key Differences
	- | Property | Heat | Temperature |
	  |---|---|---|
	  | Nature | Form of energy | Measure of hotness |
	  | SI Unit | Joule (J) | Kelvin (K) |
	  | Instrument | Calorimeter | Thermometer |
	  | Depends on mass? | Yes | No |
	- **Heat** is a form of **energy** — it is the total kinetic energy of all particles in a substance.
- Thermal Equilibrium
	- When two bodies in contact reach the **same temperature**, heat flow stops.
	- The system is said to be in **thermal equilibrium**.
