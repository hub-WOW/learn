- Atomic Number
	- Number of Protons in an Atom.
	- Symbolised by 'Z'.
- Atomic Mass
	- Protons + Neutrons in an atom.
	- Symbolised by 'A'.
- Notation
	- $_{Z}^{A}E$
	- Example: $_{6}^{12}C$
- Isotope
	- 'Iso': Same, 'Tope': Position
	- Same atomic number (Z), different atomic mass (A).
	- Similar chemical properties, different physical properties.
	- Example: ${}^{1}_{1}H,\ {}^{2}_{1}H,\ {}^{3}_{1}H$
		- Named Protium, Deuterium, and Tritium respectively.
		- Deuterium used in heavy water to cool nuclear reactors.
	- Example: ${}^{12}_{6}C,\ {}^{13}_{6}C,\ {}^{14}_{6}C$
		- Carbon-13 is used in NMR.
			- NMR is Nuclear Magnetic Resonance
			- Find structures of compounds.
		- Carbon-14 is used for Radio-carbon dating.
- Isobar
	- 'Iso': Same, 'Bar': Weight (mass)
	- Same atomic mass (A), different atomic number (Z).
	- Different chemical properties because they are different elements.
	- Example: ${}^{40}_{16}S,\ {}^{40}_{18}Ar,\ {}^{40}_{19}K,\ {}^{40}_{20}Ca$
		- All have mass number 40 but different atomic numbers.
		- These nuclei can transform into one another through beta decay.
		- ${}^{40}_{19}K$ is radioactive and decays to ${}^{40}_{18}Ar$.
	- Example: ${}^{58}_{26}Fe,\ {}^{58}_{27}Co,\ {}^{58}_{28}Ni$
		- Same mass number 58.
		- Occur in nuclear reactions and decay chains.
