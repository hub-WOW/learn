- Equation is statement of equality.
- Chemical Equation is representation of chemical reactions using symbols using formulae of reactants.
- Reactants are substances that take part in reactions.
- Products are substances that are formed after reactions.
- Rules
	- $LHS = RHS$. This is an balanced equation.
	- No atoms gained or lost. (Law of Conservation of Mass and Energy).
	- Use of '+' to seperate compounds.
- Balancing
	- Count number of atoms of an element on both sides.
	- Multiply LHS to balance RHS.
	- Multiply RHS to balance LHS.
	- Example
		- $Mg + O_2 \longrightarrow MgO$
		  
		  | Step | Equation | Explanation |
		  |---|---|---|
		  | 1 | $Mg + O_2 \rightarrow MgO$ | Oxygen atoms: LHS = 2, RHS = 1 |
		  | 2 | $Mg + O_2 \rightarrow 2MgO$ | Multiply RHS to balance oxygen |
		  | 3 | $2Mg + O_2 \rightarrow 2MgO$ | Magnesium now unbalanced (LHS=1, RHS=2), multiply Mg on LHS |
		  | Result | $2Mg + O_2 \rightarrow 2MgO$ | Balanced equation |

	  |---|---|
	  | $(s)$ | Solid |
	  | $(l)$ | Liquid |

	  | $\downarrow$ | Precipitate formed |
	  | $\ce{<=>}$ or $\rightleftharpoons$ | Reversible reaction |
	  | $\ce{->}$ | Forward reaction / produces |
	  | $\Delta$ | Heat applied |
	  | $hv$ | Light applied |
	  | $ATM$ | Pressure applied |
	  | $\uparrow\uparrow$ | Brisk effervescence (rapid gas evolution) |
